## Documentation  
## Senior Capstone: Halo Gravity Traction Load Monitoring Device

This directory contains the complete written documentation for the Halo Gravity Traction Sensor project. These files provide the technical, clinical, and engineering foundations that guided the design, development, and testing of the device.

---

## Contents

### 1. Project Requirements & Specifications  
Includes initial problem definition, stakeholder needs, engineering requirements, safety constraints, and functional specifications.  
Useful for:
- Understanding clinical motivations  
- Mapping user needs to measurable engineering requirements  

### 2. Design Reports  
Includes formal written reports submitted during the semester:  
- Conceptual design report  
- Mid-semester progress update  
- Final design report  
These documents cover research background, design iterations, risk assessment, budget, validation testing, and final outcomes.

### 3. Clinical Workflow Documentation  
Provides diagrams and workflow descriptions for Halo Gravity Traction (HGT) in hospital settings.  
Covers:  
- Patient mobility between walker, wheelchair, and bed  
- Cable tension variability  
- Traction weight safety considerations  

### 4. Testing Protocols & Data  
Includes test procedures, calibration steps, load-cell validation results, and data summaries.  
Documents the methods used to evaluate the accuracy and reliability of the load monitoring device.

### 5. Regulatory & Safety Considerations  
Outlines biomedical device standards and FDA Class I/II considerations relevant to traction monitoring.  
Covers:  
- Sensor safety  
- Electrical safety requirements  
- Patient-interaction constraints  

---

## Purpose of This Folder
This directory consolidates all written materials that demonstrate the research depth, engineering rigor, and clinical relevance of the project. It provides full transparency for how the device was conceptualized, designed, validated, and prepared for real-world use.

All documentation is original and produced as part of the UNC/NCSU Joint Department of Biomedical Engineering Senior Capstone sequence.

---

## Usage Notes
- Use PDFs for official submissions or portfolio presentation  
- Refer to design reports when reviewing CAD, PCB, or firmware decisions  
- Use workflow and testing documents to understand clinical and engineering constraints
